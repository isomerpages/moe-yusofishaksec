<h1><strong>Science Department</strong></h1>
<h4>Curriculum</h4>
<p><strong>Vision</strong>
<br>Confident, Self-directed learners who are Curious and Passionate about
the natural and physical world.</p>
<p><strong>Mission</strong>
<br>To provide a vibrant and authentic learning environment that will develop
Yusoffians as Inquirers of Science.</p>
<p><strong>Inspire, Inquire, Innovate</strong>
<br>Our Science curriculum aims to excite and nurture all students to be scientifically
literate and provide strong Science fundamentals â€“ in scientific knowledge,
practices and values, for students to innovate and pursue STEM for future
learning and work.</p>
<p>As part of our continual drive towards curriculum innovation and improvement,
our team of Science educators actively design interactive learning activities
and experiences to engage Yusoffians in the multi-dimensional learning
of Science.</p>
<p>At Y.I, students are Innovative and Inspiring Inquirers.</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Inspired</strong>&nbsp;by Science: Students are curious, appreciate
learning scientific concepts and regard the subject as relevant to their
lives.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Inquire</strong>&nbsp;like Scientists: Students are grounded in
strong Science fundamentals, think critically and possess the spirit of
scientific inquiry as they embrace the challenges faced.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Innovate</strong>&nbsp;using Science: Students extend their knowledge
towards making responsible informed decisions to create solutions to solve
many of our global challenges, to transform and improve the lives of others.</p>
</li>
</ul>

<h4>Learning Outcomes</h4>
<p><strong>Lower Secondary Science</strong>
</p>
<p>The aims of the Lower Secondary Science G2 and G3 syllabuses are to:</p>
<p>1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Cultivate studentsâ€™ appreciation of Science
as a collective human endeavour to understand the natural world, and as
a way of thinking rather than just a body of facts.</p>
<p>2.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Inspire students to make informed decisions
and take responsible actions in science-related issues that concern their
lives, the society and the environment.</p>
<p>3.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Help students develop fundamentals that
are integral to scientific inquiry and innovation, including problem-seeking
and problem-solving.</p>
<p>The aims of the Lower Secondary Science G1 syllabus are to:</p>
<p>1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Develop 21st century competencies in
students.</p>
<p>2.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Guide students in acquiring knowledge,
skills and values for application in their daily lives.</p>
<p>3.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Prepare students for future learning
and work such that they become lifelong and motivated learners; and develop
skills which are useful and relevant for them to be contributing citizens.</p>
<p><strong>Upper Secondary Biology, Chemistry and Physics</strong>
</p>
<p>The Upper Secondary Biology syllabus seeks to develop in students the
understanding, skills, ethics and attitudes relevant to the practices of
science, enabling them to:</p>
<p>1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Appreciate practical applications
of Biology/ Chemistry/ Physics in the real world,</p>
<p>2.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Deepen their interest in Biology/
Chemistry/ Physics for future learning and work, and</p>
<p>3.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Become scientifically literate
citizens who can innovate and seize opportunities in the 21st century,
and</p>
<p>[Biology]&nbsp;develop a way of thinking to understand how living organisms
work to sustain life and use the disciplinary ideas in Biology to approach,
analyse and solve problems in biological systems.</p>
<p>[Chemistry]&nbsp;develop a way of thinking to approach, analyse and solve
problems by explaining macroscopic characteristics and changes in chemical
systems through the use of sub-microscopic and symbolic representations.</p>
<p>[Physics]&nbsp;appreciate that a small number of basic principles and
disciplinary ideas can be applied to explain, analyse and solve problems
in the physical world.</p>
<h4>Key Activities,Programmes and Achievements in 2024</h4>
<p>Yusoffians are exposed to a wide range of enrichment activities and competitions
to ignite their passions and cultivate essential Future Ready Habits (FRHs).
Through participation in Science and STEM-based programmes, students have
the opportunity to deepen their knowledge while enhancing critical thinking
and problem-solving skills. Competitions encourage healthy peer interaction
and a spirit of perseverance, teaching students to handle challenges and
celebrate achievements. These activities not only expand academic knowledge
but also build essential life skills such as teamwork, leadership, and
resilience, ultimately shaping well-rounded individuals ready to excel
in future pursuits.&nbsp;</p>
